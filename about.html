About

Off the clock.

The short version: an ECE student who likes building things that talk back. The longer version is below.

I'm Dillen — a sophomore studying Electrical & Computer Engineering at UNC Charlotte, based in the Concord, NC area. I'm building toward a career in defense and aerospace engineering, and most of my free time outside of class goes toward hands-on hardware projects that push that goal forward.

It started with basic Arduino LED projects and MicroPython on a Raspberry Pi Pico, and has grown into full builds like an ESP32-based alarm system and a hardware reaction timer. I document all of it on GitHub, partly as a portfolio and partly because writing things down makes me actually understand them.

Outside of engineering, I'm into music in a pretty analytical way — I like sitting with an album and picking it apart track by track rather than just having it on in the background. I've also spent time teaching, working part-time as an instructor at Code Ninjas, which turned out to sharpen how I explain technical ideas just as much as any class did.
